What are alt coins?

    An abbreviation of "Bitcoin alternative," the majority of altcoins are simply forks of original Bitcoin code-base. Most alt-coins change only minor characteristics such as its transactions speed, distribution method, or hashing algorithm.
  • Not necessarily independent
  • Represents the true nature of Bitcoin (decentralization)

Namecoin

https://namecoin.info

Market cap: $7,836,332

Namecoin is developed by an international team of academics and volunteers working to protect free speech online and save the world from an Orwellian surveillance state. Namecoin's main purpose is to control an alternative Domain Name System (DNS) for the .bit domain. By existing outside of the control of ICANN, .bit domains can escape censorship.

Dash

https://www.dash.org

Market cap: $30,929,272

Formerly Darkcoin, Dash's major value prop is completely annonymiation -- similar to cash. Often marketed as Digital cash, hence the name "Dash". Payments are received almost instantly by the other party thanks to InstantX technology.

Ethereum

https://www.ethereum.org

Market cap: $872,765,850

A decentralized platform that runs smart contracts: applications that run exactly as programmed without any possibility of downtime, censorship, fraud or third party interference.

These apps run on a custom built blockchain, an enormously powerful shared global infrastructure that can move value around and represent the ownership of property. This enables developers to create markets, store registries of debts or promises, move funds in accordance with instructions given long in the past (like a will or a futures contract) and many other things that have not been invented yet, all without a middle man or counterparty risk.

The project was crowdfunded during August 2014 by fans all around the world. It is developed by the Ethereum Foundation, a Swiss nonprofit, with contributions from great minds across the globe.

The days of monopolies are past. We now live in a world where centralized authorities are no longer needed.

Global State

Set data-state="something" on a slide and "something" will be added as a class to the document element when the slide is open. This lets you apply broader style changes, like switching the page background.

State Events

Additionally custom events can be triggered on a per slide basis by binding to the data-state name. 


Reveal.addEventListener( 'customevent', function() {
	console.log( '"customevent" has fired' );
